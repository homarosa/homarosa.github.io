Hungry Express is a B2B app designed for delivery teams to pick up, distribute, and deliver orders,
as well as manage weekly shift scheduling.

Hungry Express specializes in group-order food delivery. Clients from the same area form a collective ordering group, enabling single orders to combine items from multiple restaurants.
Pre-orders only, and orders must be placed in advance before cutoff times. Rather than delivering to individual addresses, drivers deliver to designated pickup hubs on a schedule, where users pick up their meals to enjoy free delivery.

Workflow Overview:

  • Primary users: meal delivery drivers
  • Secondary Users: Customer support teams (coordinating between drivers and customers)

My Role

  • • Built an intuitive key workflows, including distribution management and daily task tracking.
  • • Optimized pickup & delivery flows to minimize errors and delays.
  • • Enhanced visual hierarchy, accessibility, and component consistency.

Workflow Enhancements

1. Distribution Flow Integration

  • • Problem:

    • The old version contained isolated pickup and delivery flows, but lacked critical distribution tracking. Manual meal sorting during distribution often causes:

  • ◦ Time delays
  • ◦ Order errors

  • • Analysis & Solutions:

    • Per user flow requirements, the distribution workflow must systematically organize:

  • ◦ Accessibility - distribution management is embedded within the pickup flow;
  • ◦ Service Coverage - a list shows available delivery regions
  • ◦ Regional Order Pickup Manifest - a list shows orders requiring collection per region
  • ◦ Geofenced Meal Bus Stop Locations - a list shows all available meal bus stops per region
  • ◦ Stop-Specific Order Aggregation - orders re-grouped by their assigned meal bus stop
  • ◦ Dish photos - added to the backend as a reference for drivers.

  • • Why This Works:

  • ◦ Order Verification: dishes are pre-sorted by meal bus stop in the task flow, which saves lot of driver's time.
  • ◦ Clarity: mismatched items can be flagged and reported to customer service before delivery.
  • ◦ Visual reminder: check dish photos to confirm orders.

2. Pickup Flow Redesign

  • • Problem:

    • New or substitute drivers frequently go to wrong restaurants, causing delays.
    Meanwhile, customer service relies on the app for backend coordination, requiring extensive contact details.

  • • Analysis & Solutions:

  • ◦ Integrated Navigation - add direct in-app routing to partner restaurants;
  • ◦ Restaurant Contacts - add direct in-app call to partner restaurants;
  • ◦ Reorganized order list for quick checking and category sorting.

3. Personal Task Flow Integration & Delivery Flow Redesign

  • • Problem:

    • The task list in the pick-up flow is shared among all drivers and customer service representatives. However, each driver needs to review their own daily tasks in advance to plan work effectively.
    The current delivery flow lacks an in-app navigation. Some elements either have unclear visual cues or unnecessarily duplicate features.

  • • Analysis & Solutions:

    • Based on user-centered design principles, navigation access should reside in the personal delivery tasks rather than the general delivery view; Push notifications is triggered upon arrival, therefore, it belongs to personal delivery tasks flow.

  • ◦ Combine both pick-up and delivery tasks in a single view;
  • ◦ Implement in-app navigation and calling functionality;
  • ◦ Redesign all ambiguous buttons and icons.
