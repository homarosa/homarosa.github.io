The Event Module is designed to streamline audit processes
and get to compliance faster.

User Research

Underutilization of the Event Module

Interviews conducted with end users, AKA staff users in client institutions, who schedule, and conduct audits. The results revealed a gap between available functionality and actual usage patterns:

  • • Although the Event Module is designed to streamline their workflows, routine tasks such as on-site inspection, meetings, and audits are still predominantly managed through manual processes and phone communicaiton.
  • • Rather than leveraging the integrated Event Module, staff users reported primarily utilizing only the Document Upload and Library features, mainly to facilitate paperless documentation.

Pain Points

User interview indicates that digital transformation of audit management has occurred only at the document level, not at the workflow or coordinaiton level.

My Role

  • • Focus on improving user experience for staff users creating audit events by streamlining navigation, reducing cognitive load, and ensuring seamless communication with auditors and organizations.
  • • Uncover staff frustrations stemming from overloaded coordination, excessive communications, and agenda management.
  • • Develop an interactive prototype for client demostrations, allowing uasability test.

Business Values

  • For Stakeholders: Proven ROI through reduced labor costs + high employee adoption = stronger renewal incentives.
  • For Staff Users: Staff will be more inclined to use an intuitive, efficient module—directly increasing login frequency and overall software engagement.
  • For Auditors and Audited Orgs: Clear instructions reduce communication and coordination friction, while streamlined workflows and agendas lower adoption barriers and boost working efficiency.

Analysis

User Roles:

  • • Staff(Coordinators): Responsible for planning audit event, assigning auditors, and building agendas.
  • • Auditors: Accept assignments, access schedule and resources.
  • • Audited Organizations: Prepare for audits, upload documentation, and confirm participation.

1. UX Self-review: I Conducted a self-review as a staff user,analyzing the original business workflow and uncovering fundamental design issues.

Old Version of the Event Creation Interface

  • • The staff user is required to enter a large volume of information in the early phase of event creation. However, the lack of categorization and logical grouping leads to mental fatigue and disrupts workflow continuity.
  • • The UI components are visually outdated and do not follow a consistent design system, resulting in low visual comfort and poor readability. Critical content, such as the agenda, occupies minimal screen space, reducing its visibility and overall informational efficiency.
  • • Tasks and responsibilities are distributed in a scattered, non-linear flow, resulting in heavy reliance on offline communication. The process still depends largely on the coordinator's traditional role as the central hub, due to a lack of information hierarchy and step-by-step task delivery within the system.

2. Usability Matrics Evaluation: Observe users' interactions with the system and document moments of hesitation or friction, we identified usability pain points in context.
These observations were supplemented by open-ended interviews.

  • • While the original workflow was relatively understandable, its fragmented structure offered little improving work efficiency.
  • • The core feature—the on-site audit agenda—was not prominently displayed; instead, it was placed within collapsed sections, making it difficult to check and review.
  • • As comments increase, they become cluttered and hard to review, reducing clarity and usability.

Solutions

1. Structure a step-by-step workflow: Reduce risk and communication overhead.

I map out the user journey and transformed what was previously a fragmented, staff user-driven task dispatch process into a linear, controllable flow — enabling step-by-step coordination.
Each step provides feedback, minimizing redundant actions.

2. For staff users: Enhance work efficiency.

Progressive Design:
The human capacity for processing information in the short term is generally around 5 items.
The original page presented excessive, unclassified information all at once, which not only disrupts the thought process but also overwhelms users when faced with an excessive number of input fields.
